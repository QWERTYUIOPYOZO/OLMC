Syllabus Requirements

Students learn about:

  • The response of ONE religious and ONE non-religious belief system to:
    • The concept of the transcendent
    • The human person
    • Social responsibility

Students learn to:

  • Compare the response of ONE religious and ONE non-religious belief system to:
    • The concept of the transcendent
    • The human person
    • Social responsibility

Links to Sharing Our Story

There are no specific links between this area of study and the Stage 6 Sharing Our Story syllabus.

There is a broad connection between this area of study and the Preliminary Course Core Unit; The Search for Meaning (6PC1)

It may be possible to engage with the following outcomes through this area of study:

1) reflect on the meaning of key experiences in the human life cycle from a Christian perspective

2) interpret the meaning of key scriptural texts from Christianity and other major traditions that relate to foundational human questions

3) analyse the relationship between various religious rituals and customs and religious beliefs concerning the meaning of life in a selection of major religious traditions

4) evaluate insights into the human search for meaning from psychology, faith development and spiritual writings

5) critically reflect upon the nature and role of faith and reason in the search for meaning

6) research and respond to one religious question of personal or universal significance

Approaches to Learning

The approaches suggested below indicate a range of strategies that may be undertaken to allow students to engage with the requirements of this part of the syllabus. Teachers may wish to use one or more of these approaches in addressing this area of study. The suggestions are intended as a guide only and are not exhaustive.

  • Revise the meaning of the terms transcendence and imminence

  • Make a list of significant statements relating to the concept of transcendence, the human person and social responsibility. Create a true or false check list for these points for both Christianity and Humanism

  • Draw up a table for each requirement showing areas of similarity and difference between Christianity and Humanism

  • Undertake a writing task which explores the similarities and differences between Christianity and Humanism for each of the three areas
