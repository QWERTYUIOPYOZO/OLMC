Syllabus Requirements

Students learn about:

  • The global distribution of the five major religious traditions.

Students learn to:

  • Investigate statistical data of the current global distribution of the five major religious traditions.

Links to Sharing Our Story

There are no specific links between this area of study and the Stage 6 Sharing Our Story syllabus. There are some connections between this area of study and the HSC Core Unit (6HSCC1) Religion in Australia. In completing this study, students may be able to engage with the following outcome

4) Graph the changing patterns of Christian belief and practice in Australia

Approaches to Learning

The approaches suggested below indicate a range of strategies that may be undertaken to allow students to engage with the requirements of this part of the syllabus. Teachers may wish to use one or more of these approaches in addressing this area of study. The suggestions are intended as a guide only and are not exhaustive.

  • Use groups to investigate the current situation and recent trends in the global distribution of the five major religious traditions.

  • Investigate key reasons for the current situation and recent trends in the global distribution of the five major religious traditions.

  • Discuss the relationship between materialism, secularism and the trends in the global distribution of the five major religious traditions.

  • Use current figures and trends to project the likely situation in the global distribution of the five major religious traditions over the next 25 years.
