Specimen Questions

  1. Compare how ONE religious and ONE non religious belief system recognise significant stages in life. E.g. birth, marriage.

Sample Responses

Compare how ONE religious and ONE non-religious belief system recognise significant stages of life eg. Birth and marriage. (5 marks) - 150 words

Both Christian s and secular humanis ts seek to celebrate significant stages of life such as birth, marriage and death. Though there are distinct similarities in the practical approaches of Christianity and non-religious humanism to celebrating significant stages of life, they are motivated by very different reasons. For humanists the need to recognise significant events in life comes out of the human desire to do so. Christians on the other hand, do so as a special moment of connection between the participants and God. This is regarded as the sacramental dimension of religion, because these rituals symbolise the greater reality of God's presence within the moment. Another significant difference between the way Christians and humanists celebrate significant stages in life is that for Christians days such as Lent, Sunday, Easter and Christmas have an added religious dimension. For humanists, even if they do celebrate Christmas they do so without attaching any religious significance to the celebration.
