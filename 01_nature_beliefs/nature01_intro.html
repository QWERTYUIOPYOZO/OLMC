Syllabus Requirements

Students learn about:

  • religion as a world view that:
    • acknowledges the supernatural dimension
    • has a belief in a diving being or powers beyond the human and/or dwelling within the individual

Students learn to:

  • define the supernatural dimension
  • discuss a transcendent religious worldview which has a belief in a divine power and/or powers beyond the human
  • discuss an immanent religious worldview which has a belief in a divine being or powers dwelling within the individual

Links to Sharing Our Story

This area of study corresponds closely to the Preliminary Course Core Unit 6PC1 Search for Meaning

Through undertaking this area of study students will be able to achieve the following Unit Outcomes

  • reflect on the meaning of key experiences in the human life cycle from a Christian perspective
  • interpret the meaning of key scriptural texts from Christianity and other major traditions that relate to foundational human questions
  • analyse the relationship between various religious rituals and customs and religious beliefs concerning the meaning of life in a selection of major religious traditions
  • evaluate insights into the human search for meaning from psychology, faith development and spiritual writings
  • critically reflect upon the nature and role of faith and reason in the search for meaning
  • research and respond to one religious question of personal or universal significance

Approaches to Learning

The approaches suggested below indicate a range of strategies that may be undertaken to allow students to engage with the requirements of this part of the syllabus. Teachers may wish to use one or more of these approaches in addressing this area of study. The suggestions are intended as a guide only and are not exhaustive.

  • Construct a simulation where students imagine encountering a significant experience e.g. birth or death for the first time in conscious human history. Use the reflection on the simulation to track the thought processes of the earliest human beings and their encounter key religious questions
  • Introduce the idea of supernatural through film clips of superheroes and characters from movies such Star Wars, Spiderman and Harry Potter
  • Use a brainstorm activity to identify characteristics associated with the supernatural
  • Research and record definitions of key terms such as supernatural, transcendent and immanent
  • Begin a glossary of key terms
  • Have students create a piece of art work depicting the difference between immanence and transcendence
  • Set a writing task expressing the relationship between immanence, transcendence and some of the major world religions
