Syllabus Requirements

Students learn about:

  • The contribution of ONE religious tradition in Australia to each of the following pre- 1945:
    - Rural and outback communities
    - Education
    - Public morality

Students learn to:

  • Discuss the role of ONE religious tradition in rural and outback communities pre- 1945.

Links to Sharing Our Story

This Area of Study corresponds closely to the HSC Core Unit 6HSCC1 Religion in Australia.

Through undertaking this area of study students will be able to achieve the following Unit Outcomes.

  1. research and design a timeline showing the history of Christianity in Australia.
  2. explain and give examples of the formative influence of Christianity in Australian society.
  3. analyse and critically reflect on the nature of religion and social change in Australian society including issues such as conscription and education.

Approaches to Learning

The approaches suggested below indicate a range of strategies that may be undertaken to allow students to engage with the requirements of this part of the syllabus. Teachers may wish to use one or more of these approaches in addressing this area of study. The suggestions are intended as a guide only and are not exhaustive.

  • Research the contribution of the Inland Mission of the Presbyterian Church in the establishment of the Flying Doctor service and the School of the Air.
  • Set a group task where students prepare and present reports on significant individuals such as Rev John Flynn and Bishop Nathaniel Dawes.
  • Analyse poetry of early Australia looking for indications of the search for meaning or religious values in a new country and society.
  • Construct a mind map showing the relationship between need in rural Australia and the innovative responses of religious groups.
