Glossary

Arkan al-islam

the pillars of Islam

As Salaam

meaning peace, one of the names of Allah

As-Salamu-Alaykum

traditional Muslim greeting which means peace be with you

Dar al Harb

from classical Islamic scholarship, refers to the regions of the world outside of Islamic influence

Dar al lslam

from classical Islamic scholarship, refers to the regions of the world under Islamic influence

Dar al Salaam

literally means the house of peace, refers to the ideal society

Fitnah

turmoil, disorder

Hadith

collection of the traditions of the Prophet Muhammad

Hajj

pillar of Islam - pilgrimage to Makkah

Ifq

gentleness

Iman

second station in Sufi stages of peace

Jihad

struggle, often used by extremists to convey idea of holy war

Madinah

Arabian city, first Islamic state

Makkah

Arabian city, holy city of Islam

Mushrikin

literally - polytheists, refers to Arabs who opposed the establishment of Islam at the time of Muhammad

Qital

war or fighting

Qur'an

sacred text of Islam

Salam

Arabic word for peace

Salat

pillar of Islam - requirement of daily prayer

Sawm

pillar of Islam - requirement of fasting during Ramadan

Selm

Arabic word for peace

Shahada

pillar of Islam - profession of faith

Sufism

mystical movement of Islam

Surah

section of the Qur'an

Unf

violence

Zakat

pillar of Islam - requirement of almsgiving
