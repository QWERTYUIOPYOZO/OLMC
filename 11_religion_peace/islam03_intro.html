Syllabus Requirements

Students learn about:

  • the contribution of TWO religious traditions to peace in the context of:
    • the individual - means of achieving inner peace

Students learn to:

  • demonstrate how TWO religious traditions guide the individual in achieving inner peace

Links to Sharing Our Story

There are no specific links between this area of study and the Stage 6 Sharing Our Story syllabus.

There is a broad connection between this area of study and Preliminary Course Core Unit 6PC4 A Christian Vision of the World

It may be possible to engage with the following outcomes through this area of study:

  1. Name and explain personal concerns and hopes for the world at the beginning of the Third Millenium in response to the analysis of respected individuals and organisations
  2. Identify the implications of a personal vision that integrates deeply held values and commitments.

There is also a broad connection between this area of study and the HSC Core Unit: Spiritual Pathways (6HSCC2)

It may be possible to engage with the following outcomes through this area of study:

  1. Critically reflect on and analyse the spirituality of biblical women in the Jewish and Christian traditions, and of Muslim women.

Approaches to Learning

The approaches suggested below indicate a range of strategies that may be undertaken to allow students to engage with the requirements of this part of the syllabus. Teachers may wish to use one or more of these approaches in addressing this area of study. The suggestions are intended as a guide only and are not exhaustive.

  • List and describe the qualities of contemporary or historical people who have made outstanding contributions to peace
  • Create a mind map of approaches to achieving inner peace
  • Survey examples from popular culture which focus on inner peace, compare and contrast these approaches to those identified in the Muslim tradition
  • Read extracts from the writings of Sufi mystics relating to the search for inner peace
