Summary Points

  • There are no written records from the Celts themselves
  • Archaeological evidence found on inscriptions, megalithic structures, remains of preserved humans, artefacts and the records of historians provide information about the ancient Celts.
  • Ancient Celts lived between 2000 and 50 BCE before they intermixed with other ethnic groups and lost their particular identity.
  • Ancient Celts ranged over a broad area in Europe and the British Isles and changed over time.
  • Religion, superstition, magic, rituals, quasi-scientific knowledge were not distinct from each other but were connected and interwoven into myths, stories, poems, prayers and sacred sites that provided insights into the beliefs and values of Ancient Celts.
  • Ancient Celts believed in a pantheon of goddesses and gods that were often associated with nature and natural elements. The Irish Celts had different names for the same goddesses and gods as other Celtic groups.
  • Ancient Celtic religion involved elaborate rituals that were often connected to the cycles of nature or natural events.
