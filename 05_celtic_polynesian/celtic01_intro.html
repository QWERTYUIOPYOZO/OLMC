Syllabus Requirements

Students learn about:

  • origins of the universe

Students learn to:

  • outline the principal beliefs concerning the origins of the universe

Links to Sharing Our Story

This area of study corresponds closely to aspects of the Preliminary Course Core Unit The Search for Meaning (6PC1).

Through undertaking this area of study students will be able to achieve aspects of the following Unit Outcomes

  1. Analyse the relationship between various religious rituals and customs and religious beliefs concerning the meaning of life in a selection of major religious traditions.
  2. Critically reflect upon the nature and role of faith and reason in the search for meaning.
  3. Research and respond to one religious question of personal or universal significance

Approaches to Learning

The approaches suggested below indicate a range of strategies that may be undertaken to allow students to engage with the requirements of this part of the syllabus. Teachers may wish to use one or more of these approaches in addressing this area of study. The suggestions are intended as a guide only and are not exhaustive.

  • Examine a map of Ancient Celtic sites in Ireland
  • Conduct an Internet search to find examples of creation myths from Irish Celtic mythology.
  • Reconstruct a Celtic origin of the universe based on a creation myth using images/graphics.
