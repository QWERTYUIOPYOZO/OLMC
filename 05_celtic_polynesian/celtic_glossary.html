Glossary

Allusion, allude

to refer covertly or indirectly (to a thing)

Anthropologists

the title for people who study human culture and society known as anthropology

Apologetic attitude

an attitude denoting an apology by the hunter to the animal that was killed

Apotropaic

beleifs in the power of protection from evil or evil spirits

Archaeology, archaeological

pertaining to the study of antiquities and/or the study of prehistoric remains

Astronomy astronomical

science of the heavenly bodies - planets,stars,sun,moon

Astronomical observatories

places where the movements of the Sun, moon or planets could be observed.

Beltaine

a festival to promote fertility held on May 1st, included ceremonial driving of cattle between two fires as a purification ritual also bonfires lit on hills

Blathnat

the consort of Curoi Mac Daire - her name means 'little flower'

Boand

goddess of the lower realm

bountiful

plenty of, or of a good quality and large amount of food - essential for survival through harsh winters

Bran

a story about a giant, Bran whose head is severed and carried to London on an 87 year journey where he continues to tell stories and entertain until he is finally buried

Brichta ban

women who had magical powers

Cath Maige Tuired

a famous battle saga

Classical observers

historians or historical figures that recorded their observations of the Celts during Roman times

Coffers

boxes in which things of value were stored

Common herders

those people who looked after the herds of Cattle of lower status

Conchobar

cheiftan or King referred to in the legend of the great hero and warrior Cuchulainn

conjecture

to guess or estimate

Cosmogony, cosmogonic

those people who looked after the herds of Cattle of lower status

Cuchullin/Cuchulainn

Legendary hero and warrior

Curoi Mac Daire

legendary god of the lower realm who ferries the dead across the river that separates the living and the dead

Danu

mother goddess from whom all goddesses and gods descended. Her children are the Tuatha De Danann

deification

elevation of a natural element or human to the status of a goddess or god

diabolic power

wicked or evil power

diverse

many and varied

Divinities

Spiritual beings with powers beyond those of humans such as those of the gods

Divination/divinatory

concerned with divining or discerning Knowledge by means of examining the entrails of a dead bird etc. Usually carried out by a priest or priestess

Domestic/domestication

concerned with the home, home keeping, keeping animals etc

Drochet bethad

Goidelic or 'Q-Celtic' language(which includes Irish, Scots Gaelic, and Manx) meaning 'bridge of life' reference to a mythical bridge that links earth to heaven

Druid

A priestly order of wise or 'knowing ones believed by some historians to have existed in Britain prior to the ancient Celts

Earth cult

worship of the earth and its produce

Earth god

male embodiment of the earth mother that replaced the earth mother over time

Earth mother

goddess of the earth and its produce

Elite specialists

those with special skills and high status

Elysium

the place of the dead

Engrave, engraved

cut or carve figures and or lines into metal or Ornament

Emer

wife of Cuchulainn

Eumelc

the word means 'the first milking'

Equinox Sunrise

day when hours of sunlight and dark are equal

Feth fiada

invisibility

Finn/Fion Saga

The adventures of Finn and his son Oisin and like the heroes of sagas everywhere, are mainly about hunting, fighting, and love-making. They embody much of the Celtic character - vivacity, valour, kindness, tenderness, as well as boastfulness and fiery temper.

Gallic Wars

Observations of Roman ruler, Julius Caesar

Garrotte

death by strangulation

Geas, geis, gessa

Prohibited or tabu, something that must be avoided or something that must be done for fear of negative consequences

goddesses - gods

superhuman being worshipped as having power over nature and human fortunes

graphical

depicted in visual imagery

grave mounds

sites that are the size of small hills or mounds that when excavated revealed ancient burial sites for persons of importance

Imbolc

perhaps a festival celebrating the first milking of the cows

Intercalary

a calendar month in inserted amongst the other calendar months to align lunar calendar with solar calendar

Interment

burial

Kings

great value was placed on the king whose physical and moral perfection was all important to the survival of the ancient Celts

Knowth

location of sacred site of ancient Celts known as the Boyne Valley mounds in County Meath on the east coast of Ireland

Lindow man

famous intact remains of a man from Celtic times

lochs

lakes

Loughcrew

a site in County Meath, Ireland

lower realm

the world of the dead

Lugos/Lugh/Llew

a god - the 'bright one', craftsman, musician, poet, sorcerer, warrior

Lughnasad

Irish festival held on August 1st probably was a harvest feast

lunations

reference to lunar (moon) movements ie full moon, waxing and waning of the moon - quarter

Ogma mac Elathan

Irish god of oratory who invented the Ogam Alphabet

Mabinogi

goddess mother of Llew/Lugh

Macha

goddess of the upper realm

Manannan

the god of the sea

matres

name given to the goddesses of fertility and plenty

matrona

name given to mother goddess

Medb or Aife

goddess of the middle realm

Megalithic people

ancient people who built stone structures usually for religious purposes

middle realm

the place between upper and lower realms

Mistletoe

parasitic plant that grows on trees revered by the ancient Celts as sacred and thus used in magical rituals

Morrigan

goddess of war other forms include Nemain and Babd

mythical ancestors

ancestors that stories and legends were told of, some reaching beyond the human and who had great and wondrous feats to attributed to them

natural phenomena

observed objects or facts as seen in nature

Neolithic

an anthropological term pertaining to a time in prehistory known as the later stone age when human beings lived primitive lifestyles

Newgrange

a site in Country Meath Ireland believed to an ancient Celtic burial mound

otherworld

a transcendent world beyond the material human world of lived existence

pantheon

a nation's gods

pastoral life

growing crops, established agricultural lifestyle

Pliny the Elder

An ancient historian

Priestess/Priest

sacred role, a wise healer, performed rituals including sacrifices, chants, prayers, divination, collected sacred plants and herbs

Protective symbols

Symbols of powerful humans or animals depicted on jewellery/amulets

ritually killed

killed for a sacrificial purpose such as to appease a god or to ensure fertile crops or a good harvest. The body displayed signs of a ritualistic killing and not an accidental death or death from battle wounds. At times items placed in graves with the body were also 'ritually killed'

pastoral life

growing crops, established agricultural lifestyle

Roquepertuse

an ancient Celtic site in France

Romans

Roman observers, either people who fought the Celts or recorded aspects of their society, culture and religion

sacerdotal organisation

priestly organisation such as the Druid as the leader with mysterious powers

Sacred springs

springs of water that were believed to have the protection of a goddess or god and thus had divine properties and the power to heal

Samhain

first period of the Celtic calendar year November 1st - New Year, bears traces of the harvest festival and contains some rites of ancient pastoral cults, new fire brought into houses from sacred bonfire. Also a festival of the dead, ghosts were 'fed' at this time

Scathach

famed warrior woman on the Isle of Skye who taught Cuchulainn to be a great warrior and lover

Seasonal rituals

rituals celebrating the seasons of the year

Sequana

another name for the mother goddess

Symbol/symbolic/symbolism

pertaining to which a thing is regarded by general consent as naturally typifying or representing something through analogy

Sky Father

the name given to the male god as head of the pantheon of gods

slaves

of lowest status with no freedom and worked for others in Celtic society

speculate/speculative

to wonder, to guess to talk about how things came to be

spells

special words or incantations used to charm or control another or others

superstition/s

regarding the supernatural or that which cannot be known through the senses to be knowable or credible

theory

a view held, a conception of the causal connection between facts

transgress/transgressed

to break the law or to have broken the law

Transmigration

movement through or to another stage of existence ie rebirth as a god

Transmigration rituals

rituals to commemorate movement through or to another stage in maturity or status

'threefold' death

ritual killing by three separate methods

totem/totemic/totemism

hereditary emblem of tribe or clan and the belief in its power

Trepanation

the practice of surgically opening a human skull with specially made instruments

treskele

an artistic representation of three swirls

Tuatha De Danann

the foundation myth of Irish lore meaning the children or tribe of the goddess Danu

Upper realm

the place of the goddesses and gods

Winter Solstice

time of year when the winter sun shone the shortest length of time during the day
