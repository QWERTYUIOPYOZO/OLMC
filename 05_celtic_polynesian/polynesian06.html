Content Description

How the religion provides a distinctive response to the search for meaning

Relationship to the environment

For members of Polynesian communities the myths and stories of their gods and ancestor figures provide and important mechanism of understanding their relationship to their environment.

Aligned to ocean environment

Life in Polynesian societies has traditionally been closely aligned to their ocean environment and also to their agricultural lifestyle. The images of Polynesian deities and the stories of their exploits are therefore also closely aligned to these aqua and agricultural environments. Many of the gods are depicted in elements of the environment and accordingly their exploits determine important aspects of human interaction with the environment.

Natural environment

Because of the close dependency of Polynesian life on the natural environment it follows that the traditions of the gods have significant impact on the daily life of Polynesian communities through their interaction with the environment. Examples of this interaction can be readily seen in the practices undertaken to ensure the favourable disposition of gods which will determine the fertility of the land. They can also be seen in rituals or incantations which are designed to avoid the fall out from a conflict among the gods which may result in devastating natural phenomena such as cyclones.

Harmony with divine forces

Thus through understanding the nature of the deities of their world, Polynesian communities can learn how to live in harmony with the divine forces in their natural world and ensure their own happiness and well being.

Ethical framework

Further elements in the stories of their ancestor heroes provide an ethical framework for life which enables members of Polynesian societies to have a clear grasp of what it appropriate behaviour towards others and towards other elements of the environment.

These ethical demands are not presented in terms of codes or laws. Rather they are distilled from reflection on various elements of an oral tradition which tells of the liberating exploits of ancestor heroes.

Oral traditions

The oral traditions of Polynesian societies provide an understanding of the nature of the gods and their relationship with the natural environment. Additionally they provide indications of the required ritual practices and appropriate ethical behaviour that will ensure that Polynesian people are able to live in harmony with their natural environment and the supernatural forces it contains.
