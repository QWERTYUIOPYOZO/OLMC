Syllabus Requirements

Students learn about:

Ethics

  • One of the following areas of ethical teaching in Christianity
    • environmental ethics

Students learn to:

  • describe and explain Christian ethical teachings on environmental ethics.

Links to Sharing Our Story

This area of study corresponds to aspects of the Preliminary Course Core Units 6PC2 Jesus of History, Christ of Faith and 6PC4 A Christian Vision of the World as well as the HSC Course Core Unit HSCC3 Living Texts

Through undertaking this area of study students will be able to engage with the following Unit Outcomes.

6PC2 Jesus of History, Christ of Faith

  1. Give expression to personal understandings about the person and mission of Jesus
  2. Describe and explain Jesus' response to the fundamental questions about the nature of the human person, human life and destiny
  3. Identify and analyse the role Jesus plays in enabling others to respond to fundamental human questions
  4. Study Gospel accounts highlighting the way Jesus challenged and confronted structures, authorities, people and values of his time
  5. Critically reflect on the ways Christians today challenge and confront structures, authorities, people and values

6PC4 A Christian Vision of the World

  1. Name and explain personal concerns and hopes for the world at the beginning of the Third Millenium in response to the analysis of respected individuals and organisations
  2. Explain how Jesus' proclamation of the kingdom of God was and continues to be a radical vision that is "good news" for the poor
  3. Give examples of what the Church identifies as "signs of the times"
  4. Apply principles of Catholic social justice teaching to contemporary issues crucial to the development of the world's people and the protection of its environments
  5. Identify and implications of a personal vision that integrates deeply held values and commitments

HSCC3 Living Texts

  1. Reflect on and rank the relative significance of various influences or 'moral texts' on their own and others' decision making
  2. Explain the meaning of revelation and the significance of Jesus' example and teachings in scripture and tradition for Catholics in moral decision making.
  3. Describe the role of individual conscience and the Church as moral teacher and guide
  4. Locate, interpret and apply selected texts from scripture and Church documents that describe essential features of the Christian moral life
  5. Research, analyse and make a judgement concerning a contemporary moral issue using a process of moral decision making that respects the role of both faith and reason

Approaches to Learning

The approaches suggested below indicate a range of strategies that may be undertaken to allow students to engage with the requirements of this part of the syllabus. Teachers may wish to use one or more of these approaches in addressing this area of study. The suggestions are intended as a guide only and are not exhaustive.

  • Create posters with environmental themes from scripture texts
  • Use group work to study the contributions of Francis of Assisi, Hildegard of Bingen and Pierre Teilhard de Chardin
  • Read extracts from the writings of Sallie McFague, Thomas Berry, Matthew Fox and Sean McDonagh
  • Investigate resources developed by groups such as NCCA, CEA etc.
