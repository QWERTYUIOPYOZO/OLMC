Content Description

The role of the Books of Allah and prophecy in Islam

Role of Prophecy

Prophecy is regarded as an essential element of Muslim belief. It is the way in which Allah makes known to human beings that which is required of them. In other words, prophecy is the vehicle through which the ways of Allah become revealed to human kind.

Historical tradition of Prophecy

Muslims do not believe that Muhammad was the first to receive a revelation of the word of Allah. Rather he was the last in a long line of messengers. He is regarded as the last and the greatest of these messengers and is referred to as the seal of the prophets.

Likewise Muhammad is not strictly regarded as the founder of Islam. According to Muslim belief the founder of Islam is Allah and Allah was revealed to many people before Muhammad. Those who believed in Allah and submitted to the will of Allah are regarded as true Muslims even though they existed long before Muhammad.

Messengers of Allan

The Qur'an refers to twenty four messengers who among literally thousands of others have been called by Allah and given the revelation of the message. Adam is considered to be the first of these messengers and Muhammad the last.

Nabi and Rusul

In the Qur'an two Arabic terms are used to talk about prophets or messengers of Allah. The two terms are "nabi" and "rusul". The term nabi is used to refer to an inspired prophet who has not been commissioned for a particular purpose and rusul is used to describe a messenger who has been given a particular mission.

Biblical prophets

The term rusul is given to famous biblical figures as well as to the Prophet Muhammad. The biblical figures include Adam, Nua, Ibrahim, Musa, Dawud and Isa.

Books of Allah

Among these the Qur'an gives special attention to the figures who were responsible for the handing down of significant books. The scrolls given to Ibrahim are known as "Sahifa", the books revealed to Musa are known as "Tawrah", those revealed to Dawud are known as "Zabur" and the teachings given to Isa are known as "Injil".

Books lost or altered

Collectively the revealed books of the rusul are known as the Books of Allah. Muslims believe that initially each of these revealed books contained the complete revelation of Allah, however, over time were not properly preserved and some such as the Sahifa given to Ibrahim have been completely lost.

It is believed that these books of Allah have been changed and that they no longer faithfully represent the message of Allah. Thus Muslims do not view the Torah of the Jewish religion of the Gospels of the Christian religion to be the authentic Tawrah and Injil given to Musa and Isa

Jewish and Christian scriptures respected

Thus the scriptures of the Jewish and Christian religions are profoundly respected by Muslims because they originate with the authentic books of Allah, however, given that they have been altered from their original form, they cannot be relied upon

Qur'an has universal significance

These books of Allah are also understood to have been a message given to a particular group of people in contrast to the Qur'an which has universal significance.

Qur'an last of revealed books

The Qur'an is regarded as the last of the revealed books. It gives complete guidance and has universal significance. Because it was given directly to the Prophet Muhammad, committed to memory by him and his supporters and then finally written down exactly as it was received, the Qur'an stands apart as the reliable and complete source of the revelation of Allah.

Among all the books of Allah it is the only source which is complete and unaltered. Therefore it is fundamentally reliable and trustworthy.
